Alert Settings Restore settings on this page

Enter the settings where prompted below. Click 'Save' to apply your changes or 'Back' to discard any changes. Hover over for help.

Audio Clip Volume, Internal Speaker: LowHigh 
Internal speaker volume (clips)Move the slider to adjust the internal loudspeaker volume when playing an audio clip (WAV file). Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Audio Clip Volume, External Jack:  
External jack volume (clips)Move the slider to adjust the volume delivered to the rear panel jack when playing an audio clip (WAV file). Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Audio Jingle Volume, Internal Speaker:  
Internal speaker volume (jingles)Move the slider to adjust the internal loudspeaker volume when playing a jingle. This will not affect the volume of beeps the device may generate as warnings. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Audio Jingle Volume, External Jack:  
External jack volume (jingles)Move the slider to adjust the volume delivered to the rear panel jack when playing a jingle. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Activate Local Mute Button: On Off Activate Local Mute ButtonWhen 'On', pressing the MUTE button on the device front panel will cancel any audio alert playing. When 'Off', the button has no effect other than to record an event. This setting will not affect the use of the MUTE button in confirming a factory reset. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Minimum Time Between Audio Alerts:  sec Minimum time between alertsWhen set to zero alerts will be played whenever they are detected. If set to '60' an alert will not be played more often than once every 60 seconds despite how often they might be detected. The maximum value is '999' seconds. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Duration Relay is Energised:  msec Edit relay durationFor any RF detection channel for which the realy is enabled, the duration for which it is energised is the value given here. The value can be between 100 and 65535 msec (0.1 to 65 seconds). If the relay is energised, changing the value will drop it. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Relay Inhibits RF Detection: On Off Relay functionWhen this setting is 'On' all RF detection will be inhibited whilst the relay is energised. Click 'Save' to effect any change.
Erase Audio Clips (WAV file):
Erase all audio clipsUp to 16 audio clips can be specified up to a total duration of about 80 seconds, but it is not possible to erase them individually. The only way to erase a clip is to erase all the clips and re-record those that are wanted. Click 'OK' after the warning prompt to confirm with immediate effect.
Audio Clips Compression Method: Upload an audio clips for uploadYou should select the desired degree of dynamic compression. Compression can make some audio files appear louder. If in doubt select 0dB or 6dB.
Upload Audio Clips (WAV file):
Select an audio-clip fileTo add an audio clip you must first select a suitable audio source file. The file MUST be in uncompressed WAV format and no more than 80 seconds in play-back length. The native format is 22050 samples per second mono, but stereo and other sample rate files are acceptable.

Having first selected a suitable source file you should press the upload button. The device status LED will start flashing amber to indicate the file upload which may take many seconds. Do not navigate away from this page until the upload is complete. You can test the audio clip from the 'Detector Settings' page.
